From Economics to Data Science

My path into Data Science did not start from a traditional software engineering background. It started from Economics, where I learned to think in terms of systems, incentives, constraints, growth, productivity, and measurable business outcomes. That foundation still shapes the way I approach data problems today.

Over time, I moved from economic theory and quantitative analysis into Python, SQL, Power BI, machine learning, and applied AI. In my work at PPC, I found the bridge between business and technology: turning messy operational processes into structured datasets, dashboards, automations, and AI-enabled workflows.

I am now building a portfolio that reflects that journey: from economics-informed analysis to production-minded Data Science and AI Engineering projects. My goal is not just to test models, but to design useful systems that connect data, business context, and real decision-making.
